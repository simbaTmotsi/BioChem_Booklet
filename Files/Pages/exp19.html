PURIFICATION OF EGG WHITE LYSOZYME AND TESTING OF ENZYME ACTIVITY

Preparation of Eggwhite Lysozyme

Lysozyme (trivial name): IUB 3.2.17 Systematic name: Mucopeptide N-acetylmuramyl hydrolase. Lysozyme hydrolyses B-1,4-linkages between N-acetylmuramic acid and 2 acetamido--2-deoxy-D-glucose resides in mucopolysaccharides or mucopolysaccharides or mucopeptides of a variety of microorganisms. Egg white, human ilk, tears, spleen and many other tissues including plant sources,, contain this enzyme. Its antibiotic action is attributed to the ability to destroy invading bacteria by hydrolyzing the mucopolysaccharides of the cell wall. The enzyme has been isolated in crystalline form, and the complete amino acid sequence was determined. Lysozyme has a higher Isoelectric point (Ph10.5). Than most proteins and this property will be used to purify it by ion exchange chromatography in this experiment. Enzyme assay Enzyme activity is also determined with diluted fractions. The filtered buffered egg white should be diluted 10-fold and assayed. Take 0.2 ml aliquot of the filtered buffered egg-white, which was saved and dilute it in to 2 ml. The best dilutions for the TRIS and carbonate fractions may have to be ascertained. Start with undiluted fractions. These dilutions must be made with 0.1 M Phosphate buffer must be made with 0.1M Phosphate buffer, pH 7.0. Lysozyme of egg white will hydrolyse bacterial cell walls. The assay method is based on observing turbidometric changes of a suspension of dried cell walls of micrococcus lysodeikticus. This is a specrophotometric assay in which the digestion or hydrolysis of the cell wall can be measured at 450 nm. As hydrolysis takes place, the turbidity of the cell wall suspension decreases. This decrease is used to measure enzyme activity. An abosorbance change of within 0.02 - 0.04 per minute is considred to be a good one for measuring activity.One unit of activity is equal to an absorbance change of 0.001 per minute at pH 7.0 and 25oC. The first two minutes of recorded change in absorbance are used to determine the rate of catalysis.

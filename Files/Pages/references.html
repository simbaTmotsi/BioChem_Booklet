The full reference of every citation should appear in your full reference list. When you write professionally, the target journals or publications will provide you with specific instructions of how to produce a reference list according to their house style.
Because websites can change unpredictably, do not give references to websites. When writing practical reports you should write reference following the guidelines below for the various types of sources.
  1. Scientific journal thst is published regularly.
    Surnames and initials of all authors, Name and volume number of journal (Year of publication), Full title of paper, Page numbers of paper
    For example:
    M A N Benhura and M Marume, Carbohydrate Polymers 21(1993)
    Degradation of the mucilaginious polysaccharide from ruredzo (Dicerocaryum zanguebarium) during autoclaving,13-16
    The citation in the text would be (Benhura and Marume, 1993)
  2. Book or monograph
    Surnames and initials of all authors (Year of publication) Full title of book.
    Number of edition (if there is more than one)(Publisher : City or Town of Publication)
    For example:
    Griffiths, A.J., Miller, J.H,. Suzuki, D.T., Lewontin, R.C. and Gelbart, W.M.(1996) An introduction to genetic analysis .6th edition. (W.H. Freeman: New York)
    The citation in the text would be "Griffiths et al 1996"
  3. Paper or chapter in a book or monograph
    Benhure M A and A Kilara, Enzymes, a chapter in Food Additives, Baren, A L, Davidson, P M and Salminenn S (eds) Marcel Dekker, New York, pp 425 - 476 (1990)
    The citation in the text would be "Benhura and Kilara 1993"

  4. Thesis or dissertation

    References Benhura M A N and M Marume, Degradation of the mucilaginous polysaccharide from ruredzo (Dicerocaryum zanguebarium) during autoclaving, Carbohydrate Polymers 21(1993), 13-16

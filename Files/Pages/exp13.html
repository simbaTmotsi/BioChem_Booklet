Experiment 13
		
THE SORENSEN TITRATION OF AN AMINO ACID

Due to the amphoteric nature, amino acids cannot be titrated directly using a dye indicator as a guide to the end-poing of the titration. By blocking one of the functional groups, which is possible by addiing a solution of formaldehyde prior to titration, the amino acid is converted into a derivative which behaves as a weak carboxylic acid. and can then be directly titrated in the presence of the appropriate dye indicator.

Materials

  1. 0.1M Glycine
  2. Phenolphthalein indicator, 0.1% in Ethanol (96%)
  3. M/20 NaOH (0.05 M)
  4. 40% Formaldehyde (Neutralised)

Procedure
Pipette 10ml of 0.1ml of 0.1M glycine (=amino acetic acid) solution into an conicla flask, add 20ml distilled water and 2 drops phenolphthalein indicator, and titrate this mixture with standard M/20 NaOH to a permanent pink coloration. Record the volumeused. DO NOT DISCARD THE SOLUTION IN THE CONICAL FLASK, but add to it 2ml neutralised 40% Formaldehyde, and continue the titration with the M20 NaOH until a permanent pink coloration is obtained. Record the new volume of alkali used.

Calculations and Questions

  1. Calculate the concentration of glycine in gm/litre, and give equations of all reactions involved.
  2. Indicate the ionized form of glycine at pH4 and at pH10
  3. State how this analysis could have been carried out with some success, if formaldehyde was not added at any stage.
